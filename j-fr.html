How to pronounce <j> in French

In French, the letter j is pronounced similarly to in English, but not exactly the same.

The French j has the same sound as the s in English ‘vision’.

In the International Phonetic Alphabet, this sound is represented /ʒ/, a ‘z’ with a tail.

The English j is made up of a /d/, then a /ʒ/, represented as /dʒ/.

Therefore some French words which have the same sound as English j are spelt <dj> in French:

  • Djibouti (a country in Africa)
  • Djenné (a town in Mali)

1. Practice saying Jack in English, followed by Jacques in French, noting the difference in the first sound.
In the International Phonetic Alphabet, Jack is /dʒak/ and Jacques is /ʒak/.
