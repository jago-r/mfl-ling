How to pronounce <ll> in Welsh

In Welsh, the sequence <ll> is pronounced differently from a singular <l>. The sound is not found in English, and is quite distinctive to Welsh among the languages of Europe. The sound is similar to /l/ and /s/ in different ways. It is represented as /ɬ/ in the International Phonetic Alphabet, or ‘l’ with a curl in the middle.

1. Practise saying the English words ‘lip’ and ‘sip’. Pay attention to the position of the tongue at the start of these words.

You should feel that s has a flat tongue across the width of the mouth, whereas l has a more curved tongue, so that the sides of the mouth are further away from the tongue. As you make an s sound, you should feel hissing and friction that does not happen for l.

To produce <ll>, put your tongue in the position it was in for l, so with a curved tongue and a large gap between the sides of the tongue and your teeth. You want to keep your tongue in this position, but make the actions for an s sound. The hissing and friction should come out from the sides of your mouth.

2. <ll> becomes <l> under soft mutation. Practice the following pairs:

  • Llaw (hand) – dwy lau (two hands)
  • Lliw (colour) – dau liw (two colours)

3. Practice the following common Welsh words containing <ll>:

  • Arall (other)
  • Lle (place)
  • Holl (all)
  • Llys (court)
